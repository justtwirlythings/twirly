Our Portland-based caller is experienced at teaching and leading dance events for dancers of any skill level. Whether dancers are still learning their right from left, have two left feet, or can't keep a beat, anyone can move their body and have fun! 
</p>

We're flexible - 
If you prefer live music, we can bring talented musicians and work with your venue's sound to fill the room with joy.
Recorded music is always an option and we can come to you with a big portable speaker. This option is great for practice, schools, or outdoor spaces.

<h1> Our caller</h1>
<p>
Kelly Tabor is a contra & community dance caller and organizer that has been dancing in Portland, Oregon since 2015. Kelly promotes safe and inclusive dance practices for all ages. She enjoys dances that play with momentum and works to incorporate them at an accessible level. If you’re having fun, you’re doing it correctly!

</p>

<h1>
Who can dance?</h1>
<p>
If you can walk, tell your right from your left, and mostly follow verbal instructions, you’ve got all you need!
If you still want to dance, but don’t have all of the above, let’s work something out together. 
</p>


<h2>Kids</h2> 
Dancing with younger children is always chaos, in a good way. There is no chance that we will be dancing in time to the music and that’s okay! We cover consent and safety at a surface level so everyone has a good time.
Still learning left from right? We can stick mostly to dances where that doesn’t matter much and throw in the hokey pokey for good measure.
If you’ve got a big event coming, I’m happy to come by for a couple of hour long advanced sessions with just the kids so they’ll be ready to help teach their family and friends on the big day. 
</p>

<h2>
Tweens to teens</h2>
<p>
This is such a great age for learning new things and what you like as an individual. We can do more complex moves and dance in time with the music. We cover consent and safety a little more thoroughly. Called dances (as opposed to couples dances) are great because no technical skills are needed - we’re all starting at the same level.
</p>

<h2>
Adults/All ages</h2>
<p>
Weddings, anniversaries, birthdays, graduations. You name it. Dancing is my favorite way to break the ice with large groups of people, especially the shy ones. A couple of hours of dancing will have the party ready and on the floor for when the DJ takes over, or we can keep dancing ‘til you drop.
</p>


<h1>Music</h1>
<p>
We generally dance to fiddle tunes that have a specific, repetitive count. It helps us keep time with the dance and who doesn’t smile when a fiddle is playing?
We can bring recorded music, work with your band, or suggest local bands and musicians (not just fiddlers!) that are already familiar with such tunes.
I’ll also teach a basic waltz - it’s just walking with rhythm and will come in handy forever.
</p><p>
If it’s just the caller and recorded music, we have a powerful speaker that operates on battery. If you opt for a live band, we’ll need to discuss sound when booking. Venues often have sound systems ready for just such purposes. If you have a DJ, we're happy to work with them.</p>

<h1>
Venue</h1>
<p></p>We’ll come to you, whether you’re dancing indoors or out, on a lovely wooden parquet or on grass. The dances I choose will differ slightly depending on the environment.</p>

Kelly has branched out to calling English Country dances and is glad to incorporate them at teen+ events. Kelly also dabbles in historical costuming and may well be able to dress accordingly.</p>
